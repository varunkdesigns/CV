Designing EverBee's Christmas Offer Flow

From campaign entry to purchase — optimizing a time-bound offer without friction.

RoleProduct Designer
Timeline3 weeks
PlatformWeb App
ToolsFigma · Amplitude

EverBee's seasonal offer needed to convert fast — a two-week window, a countdown-driven discount, and an audience that included both existing subscribers and first-time visitors landing from ads.

The brief was simple on paper: get people from 'saw the offer' to 'purchased' with as few decisions as possible. In practice it meant redesigning entry points, pricing display, and checkout in parallel under a hard deadline.

The problem

Analytics from the previous seasonal promo showed a steep drop-off between the pricing page and checkout — nearly 40% of users who opened pricing never started the payment flow.

Session recordings pointed to plan-comparison confusion: three tiers, four billing cycles, and a discount badge that didn't clearly show what the deal actually was.

The approach

01

Diagnose the drop-off

Paired analytics with session recordings to isolate the exact step users abandoned at.

02

Simplify the offer math

Replaced tier-by-tier percentage badges with one clear 'you pay / you save' line, tested against the original in a first-click study.

03

Design urgency without dark patterns

Used a real countdown tied to the actual campaign end time — no fake scarcity, no fabricated stock counters.

04

Streamline checkout

Cut the flow from four steps to two by pre-filling known account data and moving plan selection earlier.

The solution

The new flow leads with a single, clear offer statement instead of a comparison grid, with plan details available on demand rather than upfront. A persistent, real countdown reinforces urgency honestly.

Checkout was reduced to two screens: confirm plan, then pay — removing three redundant fields that were already known from the user's account.

Urgency didn't need to be manufactured — it just needed to be visible.
