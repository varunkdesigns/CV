Designing EverBee AI Brain — Your Smart Creative Partner

Designed an AI-powered creative assistant to help users think faster, ideate smarter, and execute with clarity.

RoleLead Product Designer
Timeline10 weeks
PlatformWeb App
ToolsFigma · FigJam · Maze

EverBee helps YouTube creators plan content that actually performs, but the research-to-idea gap was still manual and slow. Users were pulling data from one tab, brainstorming in another, and losing momentum in between.

AI Brain was built to close that gap — a creative co-pilot embedded directly in the product that turns raw research into ready-to-use ideas without breaking a creator's flow.

The problem

Creators had the data they needed but not the time or framework to turn it into content ideas. Early interviews showed people were exporting keyword reports, pasting them into ChatGPT in a separate tab, then copying results back — a workflow with at least three points of drop-off.

Stakeholders wanted an AI feature, but 'add AI' isn't a design brief. The real question was where in the workflow intelligence should live, and how much control to hand back to the user versus the model.

The approach

01

Map the existing workflow

Shadowed six creators using the current research tools to find the exact moment their momentum broke.

02

Prototype three intelligence models

Tested a fully automated suggestion engine, a fully manual prompt box, and a guided hybrid — the hybrid won on trust and speed in usability sessions.

03

Design the interaction language

Built a component system for AI responses — confidence indicators, editable suggestions, and one-click regenerate — so the tool always felt steerable, not automatic.

04

Test with real research data

Ran moderated sessions with live creator accounts to validate that suggestions felt personal rather than generic.

The solution

AI Brain surfaces inside the existing research panel rather than as a separate tool, so it reads as an extension of what creators already trust. Every suggestion is editable inline, sourced back to the data it was built from, and can be regenerated with one click if it misses the mark.

A lightweight onboarding moment sets expectations up front — what the AI can and can't do — which testing showed mattered more for trust than any amount of visual polish.

The best AI feature in this workflow was the one that felt the least like a separate feature.
